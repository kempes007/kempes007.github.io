ABOUT ME

ABOUT THE PROJECT

I am part of a team that has used the latest scientific knowledge on circadian rhythms and our bodies’ response to light, to model the effects of jet lag to produce optimal schedules to reduce jet lag fatigue. As a result, we have created an algorithm that offers individual and personalised schedules to reduce jet lag effects on any trip, guiding every traveller and notifying them, for instance, of when to sleep, seek or avoid light, or when drinking coffee would be more beneficial. The program also rates every possible trip in terms of jet lag, offering tailored suggestions on the best flights to minimise it. The algorithm has the ability to learn the user’s habits and provides big data outcomes, proving its promising potential. My role within the team is User Experience expert and Visual Designer.

THE ASK

To create a visual language/ art direction aimed at young and modern travellers who want to travel freely and effortlessly around the world. Secondly, to create an intuitive app that travellers can use as an assistant for their trip. The main functionality should be: just add a flight number and your sleeping pattern, and a tailored jet lag plan will appear on your screen. Thirdly, to design any other visual assets needed for the project.

BRANDING

I created a typographic logo where the horizontal lines are aligned above the center to represent the idea of being above the horizon, bringing the tagline “sleeping in the clouds” to the next level.



LOOK AND FEEL

The idea was to use gradients inspired by morning skies so that I could play with them in the backgrounds of the app when the customer is using the schedule while travelling, synced to a real clock time. I tried to use the gradients in a unique way that made the look and feel more “up in the sky”



THE APP

The app aimed to have a seemingless UX where every interaction is smooth. All the data input is asked through a conversational UI and the transitions between the steps are done through a soft fade. Both the UX and the transitions add a smooth look and feel to the brand. Key screens below include the account creation flow to guide users through the setup and clarify the product value proposition. All the transitions are meant to be very soft to add to the feeling of “dreamy”.



The screens below show how a conversational UI is used for all the data input; sign up and log in, adding sleeping patterns and flight numbers. The interface is embedded throughout the conversation, allowing to edit the information within the phrases and also go back by seeing the previous conversation faded.



Once the jet lag plan has been calculated, an infographic is created so that the user sees in a visual way the plan they will have to follow. Similarly to the way Google Maps works, the user will be able to press the start button to start the journey while the app follows their trip.
